Building your own tools gives you complete control over your mechanical and electrical prototyping limits. In this article, I discuss the engineering behind my scratch-built FDM 3D printer and the development of my home electrochemistry lab for plating double-sided through-hole PCBs.

1. The Kinematics and Frame Design

The printer uses a CoreXY motion structure, which keeps the heavy stepper motors stationary, allowing for high speed and minimal inertia. Features include:

  • Structural Rails: 2020 aluminum extrusions and linear guide rails form the XYZ framework.
  • Dual Z-Axis: Parallel leadscrews driven by independent stepper motors prevent bed sag and misalignment.
  • Hotend Assembly: An direct-drive dual-gear extruder paired with an all-metal hotend allows printing flexible TPU filaments.

2. Firmware Optimization (Marlin)

The control system runs on a 32-bit ARM microcontroller board. The Marlin 2.0 configuration handles mechanical parameters. Essential configurations include:

// Marlin Configuration.h highlights
#define MOTHERBOARD BOARD_BTT_SKR_V1_4_TURBO
#define X_DRIVER_TYPE  TMC2209
#define Y_DRIVER_TYPE  TMC2209

#define DEFAULT_AXIS_STEPS_PER_UNIT   { 80, 80, 400, 93 }

Implementing Trinamic TMC2209 drivers in UART mode enables sensorless homing and silent stealthChop motor operations.

3. Home PCB Fabrication & Electroless Plating

Standard home PCB methods (like toner transfer and acid etching) fail to create plated through-holes (vias), restricting designers to single-layer configurations. To solve this, I set up a home chemical copper-deposition lab:

  1. Drilling: The dual-layer CNC drills via holes into raw copper laminates.
  2. Sensitization: Submerging the board in a palladium chloride / stannous chloride solution deposits activator sites inside the hole walls.
  3. Electroless Deposition: A formaldehyde-based alkaline copper bath plates a thin (≈1-2 micron) layer of copper onto the non-conductive plastic walls of the holes.
  4. Electroplating: The board is placed in an acid copper sulfate bath to run electrolysis, thickening the traces and holes to standard 35-micron (1 oz) copper.

4. Integration

This fabrication setup enables fast, professional hardware prototyping. It supports all the custom PCBs and mechanical cases needed for bionic prostheses, wearable ML devices, and embedded AI hubs directly in-house.
